FPV Drone

Personal Project

Summer Fun

My personal drone project was born in 2018 as a way for me to do something productive over the summer. I had not had any real experience with drones or the first-person drone community, but I was eager to learn. My dad and I ordered the parts necessary to make it from scratch, and I got to work.

Making Mistakes and Growing Pains

Coming into this project I had little experience with mechatronics. I did not know how to solder, read a datasheet, and only had a vague understanding of circuitry. This project changed all of that. I practiced soldering before taking my iron to wire up my drone. I burnt plastic, frayed wires, and melted an excessive amount of flux. I fried many microcontrollers and parts with my poor soldering, but eventually got the hang of it.

The ability to read a datasheet was one of the most important skills I gained from this project. Being able to take technical diagrams and translate them into practical use is difficult but essential.

First Flights

After months of working, fighting against discouragement, and attacking the project again, the drone was finished. This drone was not a pretty creation, but it worked, and that meant the world to me.

I was also inexperienced in flying a drone. My hand-eye coordination is poor, and I am not the best with joysticks. Nonetheless, I learned the basics and started bunny-hopping my drone around empty parking lots. During one of my first long-range flights, I promptly crash-landed into the concrete, shattering part of my flight controller. While I am currently rebuilding the drone, I am much more confident in my skills because I learned from the mistakes I have made.
