Auto-Spout

Internship Work for WAGIC, Inc.

Analyzing the User Experience

I was tasked with reviewing the Auto-Spout, a Japanese product. The makers hired my company to help redesign the kitchen device and introduce it to the American market. The device was intended to retrofit old sinks with the ability to be hands free, attacking a niche population that want this function without having to buy a new fixture.

Reverse Engineering the Auto-Spout

As I dissected the Auto-Spout, I found a fatal flaw. The battery pack was sealed with a press-fit using an O-ring. Unfortunately, the cords going from the battery pack to the internals of the Auto-Spout prevented the press-fit from being effective. This is not safe for a product that is intended to be used near the water.

Additionally, I thought the form factor was clunky, and the materials felt cheap. I recommended the use of heavier plastics and included this in my own redesign.

Redesigning the Device

I learned the CAD software CREO and overhauled the body and configuration of the appliance. The internals and electronics were sound, but I needed to address how the batteries were secured. I took inspiration from the sliding battery cases found in other products like remote-controlled toys and television remotes.

Additionally, I changed the materials and ensured that my design was appropriate to injection mold. My overall report was sent to the client and utilized to inform their decisions in taking the product to market.
